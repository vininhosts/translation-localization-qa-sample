# Localization QA Notes

## Project Type

Self-initiated English to Brazilian Portuguese localization sample for a fictional AI productivity app.

## QA Focus

- meaning accuracy
- natural Brazilian Portuguese
- consistent software terminology
- concise UI text
- professional but approachable tone
- clarity for SaaS/productivity users

## Key Translation Decisions

### "Action items"

Translated as `itens de ação`.

Reason: This is a common business/productivity term in Brazilian Portuguese. It is clearer for task-management contexts than a more literal phrase such as `itens acionáveis`.

### "Owners"

Translated as `responsáveis`.

Reason: In Portuguese UI, `responsável` is more natural than a literal translation such as `dono`, which could sound awkward in task assignment.

### "Follow-up messages"

Translated as `mensagens de acompanhamento`.

Reason: This avoids unnecessary English borrowing while preserving the business meaning.

### "Settings"

Translated as `Configurações`.

Reason: Standard app/software terminology.

### "Workspace"

Translated as `espaço de trabalho`.

Reason: Common SaaS localization term and easy for users to understand.

## Tone Notes

The English source has a practical, modern SaaS tone. The Portuguese translation keeps the same tone without sounding overly informal. UI strings were kept short for button and menu use.

## Quality Checks

- No machine-translation artifacts intentionally left in the final copy.
- UI strings use consistent verbs and capitalization.
- Product name remains untranslated.
- Sensitive-information warning remains clear and direct.
- Translation avoids literal wording where it would sound unnatural in Brazilian Portuguese.
